Double Check does not and cannot guarantee that any value is correct. Format
and checksum validation can confirm that a value is internally consistent,
not that it is the right value. A verification "match" means your two
readings of your source agreed — it does not mean the source itself was
correct, current, or authentic.

What the Software does: it detects disagreement between two entries of a
value; it detects formatting and checksum inconsistencies; and it records
that you attested to a comparison.

What the Software does not do: it does not verify that a value is true,
that an account exists or is owned by the party you intend, that a source
document is authentic or current, that a transaction is authorized, or that
a destination is correct.

The Software is a decision-support tool only and is not a substitute for
